📋 What Is the Qatar Theory Test?

The Qatar driving licence theory test is conducted by the Traffic Department (Idaarah Al-Muroor) under the Ministry of Interior. It tests your knowledge of road signs, traffic rules, and safe driving practices before you can take the practical driving test.

Test Format (General):
• Multiple-choice questions (usually 30–40 questions)
• Questions in Arabic and English
• Passing mark: typically 90% or higher
• Computer-based at Traffic Department centers
• ⚠️ Confirm the exact format from your driving school or MOI before the exam

🛤️ Road Markings
⬜
White solid line: Do not cross. Lane boundary on same-direction roads.
🟡
Yellow solid double line: No overtaking in either direction. Center divider on two-way roads.
⬜
White dashed line: You MAY change lanes when safe.
🟡
Yellow dashed line: You may cross with caution (overtaking allowed if safe).
⬜
Zebra crossing (white stripes): Pedestrian priority zone — must stop for pedestrians.
🔴
Red lines at curb: No stopping or parking at any time.
🟡
Yellow lines at curb: No parking (but may stop briefly to drop/pick up).
➡️
Lane arrows: Tell you which direction you must follow from that lane.
🚦 Traffic Light Rules
🔴
Red light: STOP completely behind the stop line. Do not enter the intersection.
🟡
Amber/Yellow light: STOP if you can do so safely. Do NOT speed up to beat it.
🟢
Green light: Proceed — but still check for pedestrians and other vehicles first.
🟢↗
Green arrow: You may proceed only in the direction shown by the arrow.
🔴🟡
Red + Amber together: Light is about to turn green — get ready, but do NOT move yet.

🔄 Priority Rules
🔵
Roundabout: Traffic already inside the roundabout has priority. Give way to vehicles coming from your right inside the roundabout.
🛑
STOP sign: You must give way to ALL traffic on the major road. This is absolute priority.
➡️
Main road vs. side road: Traffic on the main road has priority. Side road drivers must give way.
🚑
Emergency vehicles: Always give way to ambulance, fire engine, or police with sirens on. Move to the side immediately.
🚶
Pedestrian crossings: Pedestrians on a zebra crossing have RIGHT OF WAY. You must stop.
⬆️
Right of way — no signs: In Qatar (as per Gulf standard), give way to traffic from the right at equal junctions.
🚶 Pedestrian Safety Rules
✅
Always stop for pedestrians at zebra crossings. It is mandatory.
🏫
In school zones, slow to 30 km/h and be prepared to stop at any time.
🌙
At night, pedestrians are hard to see. Reduce speed in residential areas.
🚌
When a bus is stopped and doors are open, slow down — people may cross.
🧒
Children are unpredictable. Near playgrounds or schools, always drive very slowly.
🚗 Lane Discipline
⬅️
Right lane = slow lane. The leftmost lane is for overtaking only — do not cruise in it.
✅
Keep left principle in Qatar: Drive on the right side of the road. Overtake on the left.
🔄
Lane change: Check mirrors → check blind spot → signal → change lane. Never change without signaling.
🚫
Lane straddling: Never drive between two lanes. It is illegal and dangerous.
📍
Arrows on road: If your lane has an arrow pointing right, you MUST turn right — no exceptions.
🅿️ Parking Rules
🚫
Do NOT park: Near junctions (within 5m), on pedestrian crossings, on yellow or red lines, in front of entrances/exits, on main roads where there is no shoulder.
✅
Parallel parking: Park in same direction as traffic flow. Leave enough space for others to pass.
🔆
Night parking on unlit roads: Use hazard/parking lights to make your vehicle visible.
⚠️
No Parking sign: You CANNOT leave your car here. Not even briefly.
⏱️
No Stopping sign: Even dropping a passenger is not allowed. More strict than "No Parking."
💡 Safe Driving Rules
📱
Mobile phone: Using a phone while driving without hands-free is illegal in Qatar. Can result in fine and/or black points.
🍺
Alcohol: Driving under the influence of alcohol is a serious criminal offence in Qatar.
🔆
Headlights: Use headlights at night, in fog, rain, and in tunnels. Fog lights only in poor visibility.
🦺
Seat belts: Mandatory for driver and ALL passengers. Children must use age-appropriate seats.
🌬️
Sandstorms: Common in Qatar. If visibility drops, slow down, turn on hazard lights, and consider stopping safely.
🔄
Overtaking safely: Signal, check mirrors and blind spots, only overtake when road ahead is clear, return to your lane without cutting in.
📏
Safe following distance: Keep at least 2 seconds gap in normal conditions. More in rain or fog.

🧠 Memory Tricks for Signs
Shape Rule
Remember by SHAPE
🔺 Triangle = Warning
🔴 Red Circle = Prohibition (NO!)
🔵 Blue Circle = Mandatory (MUST!)
🔷 Rectangle (Green/Blue) = Information
🛑 Octagon = STOP
Color Rule
Color = Emotion
🔴 RED = Danger / Forbidden
🟡 YELLOW/AMBER = Caution / Warning
🟢 GREEN = Go / Information
🔵 BLUE = Instruction / Service
⬜ WHITE = Regulation / Markings
STOP vs Give Way
The critical difference
STOP = Come to a COMPLETE stop, even if road is empty. Look. Then go.

GIVE WAY = Slow down, yield to traffic, proceed when safe. You don't HAVE to fully stop if it's already clear.
Traffic Lights Trick
The "RAG" System
🔴 R = Red = RELAX (stop completely)
🟡 A = Amber = ACTION (prepare to stop)
🟢 G = Green = GO (but check first!)
Lane Memory
Which lane to use?
Think: "Drive right, overtake left"
• Right lane = your normal lane
• Left lane = overtaking only, then come back
• Never hog the left (fast) lane

📌 Common Exam Mistakes
❌
Confusing "No Parking" with "No Stopping": No Stopping is STRICTER. You cannot even pause to drop someone.
❌
Thinking amber = speed up: Amber means STOP if safe. Never accelerate at amber.
❌
Give Way = Stop: Give Way does NOT mean full stop. STOP sign means full stop always.
❌
Roundabout priority confusion: Traffic INSIDE the roundabout has priority. Always.
❌
Blue circle = prohibited: Blue = mandatory instruction (DO this). Red = prohibition (DON'T do this).
❌
Speed limit = maximum, not minimum: Unless it's a minimum speed sign (blue with number).
❌
Dashed lines = can always cross: Only cross dashed lines when SAFE to do so, not whenever you want.

🎯 How to Read Theory Questions
Tip 01
Read EVERY word carefully
Exam questions often use words like "must", "should", "may", "never", "always". These change the meaning completely. Read twice before answering.
Tip 02
Spot the "trick" words
Watch for: "EXCEPT", "NOT", "ONLY", "ALWAYS", "NEVER". Example: "Which of these is NOT a warning sign?" — the answer is what you would NOT normally choose.
Tip 03
Eliminate wrong answers first
If you see 4 options, cross out the ones you know are clearly wrong. This narrows it to 2 options and your chance of getting it right goes up to 50%.
Tip 04
Look at the sign's shape and color first
For sign-based questions, identify the shape (triangle/circle/rectangle/octagon) and color first. This alone tells you the sign's category before reading the options.
Tip 05
The "safest" answer is usually correct
When unsure between two answers, choose the one that is SAFER. For example: "Should you slow down near schools?" — Yes is always the safer and correct answer.
Tip 06
Don't overthink
Your first instinct after studying is usually correct. Changing answers often leads to errors. Only change if you are completely certain you made a mistake.
Tip 07
Similar signs — know the difference
• Red triangle with person = Pedestrian warning (slow down)
• Blue circle with person = Pedestrian path (they must walk here)
• These look similar but mean different things!
Tip 08
Before the exam day
• Sleep well the night before
• Review your Quick Reference sheet (not everything)
• Arrive 15 minutes early
• Read all instructions at the test center first
• Stay calm — you have prepared!
